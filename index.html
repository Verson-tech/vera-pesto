Vera's Homemade Pesto

Ingredients

Previous Next
  • Vera's husband grows basil and harvests the leaves.

    • Preparation ratio is as follows:

  • 2 cups fresh basil leaves
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/3 cup pine nuts
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper

Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 1 week or in the freezer for up to 3 months.

History

Pesto is a sauce that originated in Genoa, the capital city of Liguria, Italy. The name "pesto" comes from the Italian word "pestare" which means to pound or crush. The sauce is made by pounding together basil, garlic, pine nuts, Parmesan cheese, and olive oil. Pesto is traditionally made with a mortar and pestle, but it can also be made in a food processor or blender. Pesto is a versatile sauce that can be used in a variety of dishes, including pasta, pizza, sandwiches, and salads. It is also delicious as a dip for bread or vegetables.
